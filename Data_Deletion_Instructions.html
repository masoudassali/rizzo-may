Basic Data Deletion Instructions

  1. Identify the Data: Clearly identify the specific data (files, records, backups, etc.) you intend to delete, ensuring it's no longer needed and any legal or regulatory retention periods have expired.
  2. Back Up Necessary Data: Before deletion, create a secure and verified backup of any data that might be required in the future for compliance, audits, or business continuity.
  3. Use Secure Deletion Methods: Employ secure and irrecoverable deletion methods like data sanitization, overwriting, or degaussing, especially for sensitive data on storage media that will be reused or disposed of.
  4. Verify Deletion: Confirm that the data has been successfully and permanently removed from all primary storage locations, including local drives, cloud services, and any off-site or archival backups.
  5. Document the Process: Maintain a record of the deletion process, including what data was deleted, when, by whom, and the method used, for audit and compliance purposes.
